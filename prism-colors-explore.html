Recommendation

Lead with Option A · Electric Cyan. The cyan ties directly to the "prism / refraction" brand metaphor — a beam of light passing through a prism produces cyan as one of its primary refractions, which gives the accent a built-in narrative. Cyan also reads as distinct from gold, so the platform can use cyan for "active state / progression / system" and gold for "value / brand / financial figures" without the two ever competing for the same job. Champagne ivory (Option B) is beautiful but it sits too close to gold — the eye reads them as one warm family, which weakens the visual hierarchy on data-dense screens like the admin deal record.

Operator decision points

Five questions before we touch a live portal

01
Pick A or B?Electric cyan (refraction narrative, strong contrast vs gold) or champagne ivory (warmer, more harmonious with gold but less differentiated)?
02
Gold — primary accent, or demoted to "financial values only"?Today gold does everything: nav active, CTA, IRR figures, brand mark. In Prism Colors, the bright accent (cyan/champagne) takes over CTAs and active state, gold becomes reserved for monetary figures and the brand mark. Confirm this split.
03
Navy depth — #0A1228 (cooler) or #0E1A36 (warmer)?The two options use slightly different navies. The cooler navy feels more "cinematic / institutional"; the warmer one feels more "old-world private bank". Pick one regardless of accent choice.
04
Royal purple — surface 2 or accent only?Currently used as a chrome/surface element (left rails, hero glow). Alternative: pull purple into the accent role (e.g. badges, status pills) and drop the bright accent entirely. Three-color scheme is calmer; four-color is more "prism".
05
Rollout order if approved — investor first, or all three at once?Investor portal is most visible to the customer. Advisor + admin can lag by a week. Or we can ship all three together to avoid mid-flight inconsistency. Pick one.
