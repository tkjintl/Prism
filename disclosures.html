§ 05

Conflicts of interest

TACC operates on the principle that conflicts must be disclosed, governed, and visible on the register, not hidden in footnotes.

TACC-sponsored deals

Where TACC, an affiliate, or a connected entity acts as sponsor, general partner, fund manager, or principal on a transaction, that transaction is flagged on the deal register with the label "TACC-sponsored". Members can identify these transactions before submitting an indication of interest. Pricing, fee structure, and economic terms of TACC-sponsored deals are set out in the offering documents.

Affiliated advisors

Where a deal advisor on the Platform is an affiliate of TACC, related to a TACC director or employee, or has a material commercial relationship with TACC outside the Platform, that relationship is disclosed on the advisor's profile and on each transaction the advisor brings.

Carry-share and economic alignment

TACC may participate in carry, success fees, or revenue-share arrangements with sponsors and advisors. The existence and broad shape of such arrangements — but not necessarily the specific commercial terms, which are confidential — are disclosed on the relevant deal record. A summary of TACC's standing economic-alignment arrangements is available on request to prism@theaurumcc.com.

Allocation

Where demand exceeds available allocation, TACC allocates on a documented basis taking into account fit with sponsor preferences, depth of relationship, and order of indication. The allocation methodology is recorded in the audit log for each transaction. TACC does not allocate to itself, its directors, or its employees on terms more favourable than those offered to Members.

Personal account dealing

TACC personnel are subject to a personal-account-dealing policy that restricts trading in instruments connected to Platform transactions during defined sensitivity windows.

§ 06

Marketing restrictions

Communications about specific transactions are made only to admitted Members who qualify as accredited or institutional investors under the Singapore Securities and Futures Act and, where relevant, as accredited investors under Rule 501(a) of US Regulation D. The Platform's public-facing pages contain no offer of securities and are not solicitation material.

The Platform does not advertise specific deals, returns, or sponsors to the general public, does not solicit retail subscribers, and does not operate as a public crowdfunding portal. Marketing emails are sent only to Members who have provided consent and may be unsubscribed at any time.

Cross-border communications are restricted in accordance with the securities and marketing laws of the recipient's jurisdiction. The Platform is not for distribution where prohibited.

§ 07

Complaints

Members and other parties may submit complaints in writing to prism@theaurumcc.com with the subject line "Complaint". TACC will acknowledge within five (5) business days and substantively respond within thirty (30) days. Where a complaint relates to a regulated activity and is not resolved internally, the complainant may escalate to the Monetary Authority of Singapore.
